Health

Miniature Schnauzer Health Concerns

The conditions this breed can be prone to, how long they live, and the preventive care that keeps them thriving.

Common Miniature Schnauzer Health Conditions

Miniature Schnauzers are generally healthy, but like every breed they have a few conditions worth knowing about so you can catch them early.

Health conditions the Miniature Schnauzer can be prone to
ConditionWhat it isSigns to watch for
PancreatitisInflammation of the pancreas, sometimes linked to high-fat dietsVomiting, low appetite, belly pain
Bladder & kidney stonesMineral stones in the urinary tract the breed is prone toStraining, blood in urine, frequent urination
HyperlipidemiaHigher-than-normal fat levels in the bloodOften none; usually found on bloodwork
CataractsClouding of the eye’s lens that can affect visionCloudy-looking eyes, bumping into things
Comedone syndrome“Schnauzer bumps” — small crusty bumps along the backBlackhead-like bumps, occasional scabbing

Lifespan and Aging

With good care, Miniature Schnauzers typically live 12 to 15 years. As they age, keep an eye on weight, dental health, and vision, and adjust exercise to match their comfort.

Preventive Care Checklist

  1. Schedule an annual wellness exam — twice a year for seniors.
  2. Keep up with dental cleanings and at-home tooth brushing.
  3. Feed a measured, balanced diet and avoid fatty table scraps.
  4. Provide daily exercise to maintain a healthy weight.
  5. Stay current on vaccinations and parasite prevention.
